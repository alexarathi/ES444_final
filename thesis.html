Thesis: In the long term only impactful investments survive. While there may be temporary opportunities in the market for expliotive businesses, external stakeholders eventually close these opportunities. Unfortunately, quarterly earnings and CEO turnover creates and environment for management to be incentivsed to focus on short term not long term. The solution is to provide patient capital to allow management to prioritize long term over short term profit.

Let's follow Tesla ... if you bought $10,000 worth of Tesla stock in 2010 it would be worth $1,300,000 today

2003

Tesla's journey begins and is founded by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning in Palo Alto, California. There was much questioning going on - "who would try to disrupt the automotive giants? They will never win."

2004-2005

Elon Musk leads the company's $7.5 million Series A financing round and becomes chairman of the board, Elon Musk injects $13 million more into the company in a Series B round during the development of the yet-announced Tesla Roadster.
The company is not profitable.

2006

Elon Musk and Technology Partners raise $40 million in a Series C round for Tesla.
The company is not profitable.

2008

Tesla makes the Roadster and announces the Model S at a sales price of $50,000. Musk is now the CEO and has cumulatively injected $70 million of his money into Tesla.
The company is far from profitable

2009

Tesla raises $40 million in a debt-financing round and narrowly escapes bankruptcy. Tesla takes a $465 million loan from the United States Department of Energy and repays its loan by May 2013, 4 years later.

2010

On June 29, Tesla goes IPO at $17 per share

2011-2013

Tesla announces its factory in Freemont, CA and unveils the Model X while selling the Model S. One of Tesla's Model S catches on fire and there is large backlash. New Jersy bans sales of Tesla.
The company is still not profitable but is still recieving millions in funding.

2015

Chris Christie reverses the previous order and Tesla announces some complex product launches.
The company is still not profitable, but they reach a milestone of 100,000 units sold of the Model S ...

2016

With this heightened energy, 13 years later, Tesla finally reveals its first car (Model 3) which is aimed for mass market consumers.

2017

Tesla is ranked in the consumer reports as the top American car brand, and a leader in the world.
The company however is not profitable but is still recieving millions in funding.

2018

Tesla misses projection figures and investors dump the stock as it lost $12B. They were producing cars at 1/3 of the speed and were unable to meet expectations. Elon Musk starts to say some questionable things and is charged with securities fraud by the SEC.
The company is not profitable, and despite the CEO acting out, they are still recieving millions in funding.

2019

Musk reveals the Cybertruck and claims to have gotten 250,000 orders.
The company is finally somewhat profitable

2020

Stock surges 700x, cars are in high demand, the industry is booming, and the environment is happier. Traditional, incumbent car makers such as Ford, GM are making cars, hundereds of battery suppliers have emerged, people are following suit and starting to care about sustainability
