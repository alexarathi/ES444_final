Patient capital is the first step. These are some things that can support that.

PROBLEMS/ CHALLENGES MITIGATIONS
1. Educating Investors & the Public The term "impact investing" has become popular, even trendy. One important way to help the trend become a constant is through education itself. It is said that impact investment firms significantly outperformed traditional bets during the coronavirus pandemic.Encouring folks to buy from sustainably made brands and be more meaningful with the way they purchase is the other part of the cycle. Educating both investors and the general public. Supporting local organizations and crowdfunding leads to a healthier investing ecoystem.
2. Government Incentives Smart public policy can galvanize impact investing. In order for there to be a strong delta, a much more intentional and proactive partnership between both the government and the private sector is needed.
    - Remove regulatory barriers
    - Increase the effectiveness of government programs
    - Provide incentives for new private impact investments
3. Data Clarity Similar to how chocolate bars have labels on calorie count and sugar content, why don't companies have to have the same for clothes, softwares, etc.? Having labels describing how much CO2 was emitted or what workers were paid to make the product. Creating a platform that shows how sustainably managed companies are would allow for a lot more transparency
4. Create one Universal Metric to define "Impact" One of the toughest parts about impact investing is defining what impact actually means. There are numerous ways to measure it which often disuades people from thinking too much about it, or only caring about a certain cause. Creating universal definitions for the term is essential to change.
5. Encourage companies to stay "Mission aligned" Urge business owners and investors to follow the mission first, not the money. Acumen capital.
